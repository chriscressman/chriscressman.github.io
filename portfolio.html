Order Pricing Diagrams

Workarea's architect and lead engineer told me, "I don't know how you're going to explain pricing", referring to the complexity of the topic. To manage this complexity, I created a large number of diagrams. These diagrams worked backward from the user interface to explain a particular price, because this is the task an engineer is most commonly asked to perform regarding pricing. The diagrams deconstruct the UI into "pricing totals" API calls, which are each derived by summing "price adjustments", which are themselves derived from "pricing calculators". By reducing pricing to these three concepts, I made pricing accessible to all Workarea engineers.

Learn Rails Initialization

Rails gives you a lot of places to put initialization code, and the order in which they run is only partially documented. Once, Rails initialization order caused a problem for us twice in the same week, so I decided I wanted to solve this problem. Sometimes the best way to learn is by doing, so I created an application which resembled a real-world Workarea application (with several plugins installed). Then I inserted logging code all throughout the application and plugin to log every possible place initialization could be run.

The resulting log provided a definitive answer to the order in which all Rails initializers are run. I shared this with the team. This probably took me a couple hours at most and solved an issue that had been hurting us for a while.

I like to learn through experimentation and exploration. I regularly create disposable applications to answer specific questions about the software. In this case I happened to keep the app as a reference for me and the team, which is good so I can share it with you here.

Baseball Score Sheet

I played a lot of sports growing up, but as an adult I had the pleasure of being on a baseball team with my two younger brothers, and my dad was the coach. Having been removed from sports for many years, I wasn’t a very good player, but I had a lot of fun keeping score at each game. However, the store-bought score sheets we used were a little too “busy” for me. So, I created my own–in HTML and CSS of course!

Baseball Score Sheet

While a nice score sheet indeed, I include this here mostly to bring up my background in organized sports. Team sports, particularly demanding year-round sports, likely played a large role in shaping who I am today. I attribute many import life and business lessons to my experiences on these teams, such as:

  • Determination: “showing up” (literally and figuratively) every day, even when it’s hard or you don’t want to
  • Rapid learning and skill development: drills, repetition, pushing beyond your comfort zone
  • Reflection: learning from your mistakes; seeing them as opportunities to improve
  • Mission: sharing a vision with others who are equally determined; being a part of something larger than yourself
  • Achievement: trusting the process; watching small gains compound into great achievements
